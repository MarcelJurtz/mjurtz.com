Todays article is kind of different from the others. If you're reading this, I'm quite sure that you are in a similar situation to me. As a software developer with several sideprojects and maybe even some social interaction, your days are usually quite planned.

One thing I recognized to be really important to me the last months, is physical exercise.

However, when you're busy with other things you're quite likely to drop a days exercise to make more room for something that seems to be more important.

The thing is, that exercising helps (at least in my case) to focus. So after exercising, I'm able to work more efficient and actually get stuff done.

However, while the result is always the same, it's often really hard to stop working on a project for exercise. Consistency is key. You've probably read this more than one time earlier. The only thing that worked for me to keep going, is consistency.

But how do you build consistency in the first place? In my case, I wanted to start running. I hated running. Really. But I wanted to prove myself that I can do this, even if it's uncomfortable. And I have to admit that it's fun now.

To get this far, I can really recommend two apps to help you doing the same. One is a habit tracker. It basically allows you to define habits and how regularly you'd like to do specific tasks. Each day, you tick off the parts you achieved that day.
