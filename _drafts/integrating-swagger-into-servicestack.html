In Â my last post, I covered the basics of building RESTful APIs using ServiceStack. This article builds on top of the previous one and adds Swagger for documenting the API.

First, the swagger plugin needs to be installed (NuGet) and added:

Plugins.Add(new SwaggerFeature());

After that has been done, you can add attributes to your services to generate metadata for swagger.

[Route("/hello/{name*}", Summary = "Says hello to you", Notes = "Further description")]

After launching your app, you can view Swagger UI under /swagger-ui. The interface shows information about each route
